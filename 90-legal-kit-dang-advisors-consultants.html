FOUNDER STOCK PURCHASE — IP CONTRIBUTION SIDE LETTER (Dang version)

This IP Contribution Side Letter ("Side Letter") is made as of [DATE] between BrandrAI, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company"), and Dang Tien Ngoc, an individual resident of Vietnam ("Founder"), in connection with the Founder Stock Purchase Agreement of even date herewith between Company and Founder (the "SPA") and the Co-Founder Term Sheet dated April 2026 (the "Term Sheet").

1. Background.

Pursuant to the SPA, Founder is acquiring 400,000 shares of the Company's Common Stock (the "Shares") at a purchase price of $0.0001 per share, for an aggregate cash purchase price of $40.00 (the "Cash Consideration"). This Side Letter sets forth the additional consideration provided by Founder, namely the assignment of pre-formation intellectual property, and clarifies the separate treatment of Founder's pre-formation cash investments in buildx.

2. Assigned Intellectual Property ("Assigned IP").

Founder hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Company all right, title, and interest in and to the following, collectively constituting the Assigned IP:

2.1 — buildx product and business (the primary buildx asset transfer)

  • All source code of the buildx product, including but not limited to the AI-powered mobile application generation engine, template library, theme editor, preview/rendering subsystem, and all client-side and server-side components
  • All domain names controlled by Founder in connection with buildx, including but not limited to buildxone.vercel.app and any registered custom domains (e.g. buildxone.com, if registered)
  • All brand assets of buildx including name, logomarks, visual identity, marketing copy, screenshots, and product documentation
  • All trademark rights in the buildx name and marks, if any
  • All customer relationships and user accounts, if any, associated with the buildx product (Founder represents that buildx is currently pre-launch with zero paying customers as of the date hereof)
  • All user data, analytics, and operational records associated with buildx, subject to applicable data protection laws
  • All vendor/contractor relationships, hosting configurations, third-party integrations (including but not limited to WordPress, WooCommerce, Shopify, Magento integration code and configurations), and operational infrastructure

2.2 — BrandrAI core product contributions

  • Source code for the app.brandrai.com platform, including all code authored, committed, or co-authored by Founder prior to the date hereof
  • Architectural design, system specifications, and technical documentation of the COA verification pipeline, Metrc integration service, AI verification service abstraction, and real-time broadcasting subsystem
  • All rights as named co-inventor on the patent disclosures 74 (Cannabis COA Verification), 75 (Proof primitive), and 75b (BrandrAI Sites)
  • Any related software libraries, utilities, or derivative works developed in the course of pre-formation work on the Company's product

2.3 — EXPLICITLY EXCLUDED from Assigned IP (Founder retains full ownership):

  • Appcheap (rnlab.io) — all code, brand, customers, and revenue
  • Pre-existing CodeCanyon-published products
  • Personal open-source contributions
  • Work performed for clients other than the Company prior to the date hereof

3. Consideration.

The consideration for Founder's assignment of the Assigned IP is the Shares acquired under the SPA, in conjunction with the Cash Consideration. Founder and Company agree that the Assigned IP has material value to Company, particularly the buildx product which will be developed and operated as a product line within Company.

4. Pre-Formation Cash Investments in buildx.

Founder has represented that Founder personally funded approximately fifteen thousand U.S. dollars ($15,000) in pre-formation business expenses in connection with the development of buildx, including but not limited to cloud hosting, third-party services, design services, domain registrations, and related development costs. These cash investments are separate from, and not part of, the consideration for the Shares. Company will reimburse Founder for verified pre-formation business expenses pursuant to a separate Pre-Formation Expense Reimbursement Memorandum, payable from Company funds after establishment of Company's bank account. Such reimbursement is compensation for business expenses advanced on behalf of Company, not additional equity or compensation.

5. Representations and Warranties.

Founder represents and warrants that: (a) Founder owns the Assigned IP free and clear of any liens, encumbrances, or third-party rights; (b) Founder has full right, power, and authority to make the assignment contemplated by this Side Letter; (c) to Founder's knowledge, the Assigned IP does not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party; (d) Founder has not previously assigned, licensed, or conveyed any portion of the Assigned IP to any other person or entity; (e) buildx is currently pre-launch with no paying customers, no outstanding customer contracts or service-level obligations, and no outstanding investor or other equity claims against the buildx product or business.

6. Further Assurances — buildx Transfer Mechanics.

Founder agrees to execute such additional documents and take such additional actions as Company may reasonably request to perfect the buildx asset transfer, including within thirty (30) days of the date hereof: (a) transferring buildx domain names to Company-designated registrar account; (b) transferring buildx code repositories to Company-designated GitHub/GitLab organization; (c) transferring any associated vendor accounts (hosting, third-party APIs, analytics) to Company ownership or granting Company administrative access; (d) providing operational documentation sufficient for Company engineering team to continue development of buildx; (e) executing any trademark assignment forms required.

7. Relationship to CIIA and Term Sheet.

This Side Letter is complementary to, and not in lieu of, the Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement between Founder and Company, and the Co-Founder Term Sheet dated April 2026. In the event of conflict between the Term Sheet and this Side Letter with respect to the identification and assignment mechanics of the Assigned IP, this Side Letter controls.

8. Governing Law.

This Side Letter is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

ADVISOR AGREEMENT

This Advisor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on [DATE] between BrandrAI, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company"), and [ADVISOR FULL LEGAL NAME], an individual ("Advisor").

1. Advisory Services.

Advisor agrees to provide advisory services to Company in the following areas (check applicable, customize as needed):

  • Strategic direction, product, and go-to-market advice
  • Industry introductions and relationship-building
  • Recruiting assistance and team feedback
  • Investor introductions and fundraising support
  • Ad-hoc problem-solving on CEO's request
  • [CUSTOMIZE for specific advisor skillset]

Advisor commits to the following time and engagement level:

  • Monthly check-in call of approximately 30-60 minutes
  • Reasonable availability for ad-hoc calls/emails (response within 3-5 business days)
  • 2-4 meaningful introductions per quarter
  • Total commitment: approximately 4-8 hours per month on average

2. Advisory Period.

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for twenty-four (24) months (the "Advisory Period"), unless earlier terminated as set forth in Section 7. At the end of the Advisory Period, the parties may mutually agree to extend this Agreement.

3. Compensation — Equity.

In consideration for the Advisory Services, Company grants Advisor a stock option to purchase [AMOUNT] shares of Company's Common Stock (representing approximately [0.25%-1.0%] of Company's fully-diluted capitalization as of the Effective Date) at the fair market value per share on the date of grant (the "Option"). The Option vests over twenty-four (24) months in equal monthly installments, with no cliff, commencing on the Effective Date. The Option is granted under Company's Stock Plan, subject to the terms of such plan and a separate Stock Option Agreement.

Advisor typical equity ranges by level:

LevelTypical equityWho this is

4. Expense Reimbursement.

Company will reimburse Advisor for reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses pre-approved in writing by Company's CEO. Advisor shall submit expense reports with receipts within thirty (30) days of incurring the expense.

5. Confidentiality.

Advisor agrees to hold all non-public Company information in confidence for a period of three (3) years from disclosure (perpetual for trade secrets). Advisor shall not use Company confidential information except for purposes of providing Advisory Services and shall not disclose it to any third party.

6. Non-Solicitation.

During the Advisory Period and for twelve (12) months thereafter, Advisor agrees not to solicit for hire any employee, contractor, or founder of Company.

7. Termination.

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice. Upon termination: (a) Advisor continues to vest any then-vested portion of the Option; unvested portions are forfeited; (b) confidentiality and non-solicitation obligations survive termination; (c) Advisor returns or destroys all Company materials.

8. IP Assignment.

Any work product created by Advisor specifically for Company in the course of providing Advisory Services is assigned to Company. Advisor's pre-existing intellectual property, and IP created outside the scope of Advisory Services, remains Advisor's property.

9. Independent Contractor.

Advisor is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner of Company. Advisor is responsible for their own taxes.
