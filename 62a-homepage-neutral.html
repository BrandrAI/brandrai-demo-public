Start with why

No one trusts what they see online anymore.

And that changes every purchase decision your customers make. This didn't happen in 2026. It's been building for six years — and the curve just went vertical.

2019
Pre-COVID
~11% of retail online. You trusted the store, the shelf, the review.
2020-22
COVID shift
+$1T e-commerce. Resale grew 5x faster than retail. DoorDash, StockX, Whatnot explode.
2023
TEMU + knockoff era
200M+ TEMU users in the US. ~40% of Amazon listings flagged as counterfeit. Reddit becomes the #1 trust source.
2024
AI slop flood
GenAI goes mainstream. ~1,000 AI news sites. Fake reviews, fake photos, fake founders. Deepfakes +3,000% YoY.
2026 — now
Default disbelief
71% of consumers can't tell AI from human content. If it's online, assume it's fake until proven real.

How trust broke, in order.

1. Reviews broke first

63% of consumers now trust Reddit threads + peer posts over brand claims. Amazon reviews — the gold standard in 2015 — are now assumed to be bought, bot-generated, or paid-for. "Verified purchase" stopped meaning anything.

2. Product photos went next

AI-generated product images are indistinguishable from studio shots. TEMU clones real brands in 48 hours. Your packaging can be on a counterfeit SKU by tonight — with better photos than you paid a studio to shoot.

3. Then founders themselves

Deepfake CEO videos endorsing fake products. Deepfake podcast interviews. Deepfake testimonials. Consumers can't tell — and neither can retailers, banks, or payment processors reviewing your brand.

Universal pattern Every vertical with real value to protect

Brand collision is the shared disease.

In every category that matters — luxury, sneakers, supplements, skincare, wellness, collectibles, specialty food, or anything regulated — the same pattern repeats. A few "safe-sounding" prefixes cluster hundreds of brands together. Knockoffs use near-identical names. Consumers, retailers, and banks can't reliably tell who's who. Trust and LTV collapse.

Retail: consumer confusion

Customers buy the wrong brand because the name sounds like another. Repeat-purchase rate drops when your customer can't reliably re-find you on a shelf or in a search result.

B2B: buyer + distributor confusion

Retail buyers misorder SKUs because similarly-named companies look identical in a catalog. CRMs can't tell "Leaf Labs" from "Leaf Company." Different companies, same shelf.

Legal: weak trademark coverage

Many high-growth verticals can't federally trademark, can't afford to enforce when they do, or face state-by-state patchwork. Thousands of disputes go unresolved annually.
