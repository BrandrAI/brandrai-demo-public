2 · The insight

We already built the hardest part — for a narrower market.

The COA verification engine we shipped in April 2026 does five things in sequence:

  1. Accepts a document (PDF, image, data file) at an exact moment in time
  2. Computes a cryptographic SHA-256 hash bound to a trusted UTC timestamp
  3. Runs multi-modal AI analysis to extract structured metadata (authors, dates, entities, numbers)
  4. Scores the document for authenticity signals vs. known-good examples
  5. Produces a public, tamper-evident verify page with a QR code and signed certificate

Strip off “cannabis COA” from the above list. What remains is a general-purpose authenticity infrastructure that solves the same problem across every knowledge-work vertical:

  • Academia: prior-art timestamps for dissertations, datasets, peer-review submissions
  • Patent / IP: invention-date proof for engineering notebooks, trade-secret disclosure trails
  • Creative: authorship timestamps for screenwriters, novelists, musicians, photographers, digital artists
  • Industrial: policy-in-effect proofs, engineering spec versioning, contract tamper detection
  • Legal: chain-of-custody for digital evidence, deposition exhibits, affidavits

The underlying “is this real? when? verified by whom?” problem is identical across every one of these. We solved it once for cannabis because that's where willingness-to-pay was highest and regulatory pressure was most acute. We did not realize, until recently, that we had solved it for everyone else at the same time.

5 · Why we win

Everyone else picked a lane. We ran the whole road.

Authenticity is a crowded space if you look at it a slice at a time. But nobody has stitched the full stack together the way we have. Each competitor below owns exactly one primitive; we own the bundle.

vs. OpenTimestamps / Bitcoin anchoring

Technically capable

They do: free, immutable timestamp via Bitcoin. Developer-only UX. No certificate, no verify page, no metadata, no legal packaging. We do: all of that plus the timestamp. The engineer uses OTS; the screenwriter, researcher, and lawyer use Proof. Bigger audience.

vs. US Copyright Office / WGA registration

Legally accepted, slow

They do: slow (3-month average for Copyright Office), niche (WGA is scripts only), expensive ($20-65 per registration). Full legal certification. We do: faster (30 sec), cheaper ($5), broader (any format). We're not a replacement for formal registration — we're the cheap, instant, continuous layer underneath. Writers do Proof timestamps weekly, Copyright Office once.

vs. DocuSign / Adobe Sign CLM

Owns signing

They do: signing, routing, envelope lifecycle, compliance. $600-5K/mo per business. We do: cryptographic hash + tamper evidence + AI analysis. Complementary, not competing — DocuSign integrates Proof for an upgraded tamper-proof tier. Partnership play more than replacement.

vs. Vanta / Drata / Secureframe

Owns SOC 2 workflow

They do: compliance frameworks, evidence collection workflow, auditor coordination. We do: the underlying cryptographic evidence layer. Every control document they track could have a Proof timestamp auto-applied. API partnership territory.

vs. Stampery / WordProof / Surety (historical)

Tried, mostly dead

Crypto-first timestamping startups from the 2015-2019 era. Most dead or pivoted. Failed because they led with “blockchain” not “solve your problem.” No AI, no user-friendly certificates, developer-only UX. We learn from them: lead with the use case, not the tech. Crypto is the plumbing, not the product.

The bundled advantage: every one of these competitors is a single-primitive solution. We combine cryptographic timestamp + AI metadata extraction + court-ready certificate + public QR verify page + brand integration. No one else does all five. Users don't want five vendors; they want one that solves “prove this is real, in a way I can actually use.”

7 · Risks & honest unknowns

What could break this.

1

Dilution of the cannabis story before cannabis pays.

Risk: talking too much about Proof pre-Series A makes investors wonder if we're focused. Mitigation: keep Proof internal + waitlist-only until post-May 1 launch, post-Ohio traction. Memo goes to advisors now; product page launches with traction. Don't lead with Proof until cannabis ARR is >$1M run rate.

2

“Horizontal is harder than vertical.”

Risk: classic SaaS wisdom says pick a vertical, dominate it, then expand. Proof is horizontal. Mitigation: we're NOT going horizontal on day one. Cannabis is still the go-to-market vertical. Proof is the technical bundle that unlocks adjacent verticals one at a time — each one a separate GTM motion using the same engine. This is the Shopify playbook: built for clothing merchants first, then everyone.

3

Legal defensibility of the timestamp.

Risk: we're claiming Proof timestamps are court-accepted. In practice that depends on jurisdiction, case specifics, and whether opposing counsel challenges the method. Mitigation: pair with optional on-chain anchoring (Q3 2026) for strongest evidentiary weight. Engage a legal advisor to produce a whitepaper on admissibility. Offer an “enhanced” enterprise tier that includes notarized attestation for highest-stakes use.

4

Existing platforms build this as a feature.

Risk: DocuSign, Adobe, Vanta could add cryptographic timestamping as a bullet on their own roadmap and absorb the use case. Mitigation: speed. Launch Proof Q3 2026 while incumbents are still focused on their core. Build the brand. Become the default answer on authenticity so when DocuSign wants to launch it, they partner with us instead. Alternately: sell/license the engine.

5

Cost of hashing + storage + AI at scale.

Risk: $5/stamp pricing may not leave room for AI analysis cost (GPT vision is ~$0.10-0.50/call) + permanent storage. Mitigation: tier AI analysis so low-tier = hash only + basic metadata; pro tier = deep AI. Storage is near-free for small docs. At $49/mo creator tier with 50 stamps, gross margin modeled at 65-75%.
