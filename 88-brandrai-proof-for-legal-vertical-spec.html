1 · Why legal is the second vertical

Lawyers speak our language. They pay premium. They refer to each other.

Why legal wins as #2
  • Natural product-market fit — cryptographic proof solves active evidentiary pain
  • Federal Rules of Evidence 902(13)/(14) directly validate the primitive
  • Premium ACV — 50-seat firm = $10-25K/mo vs. single operator $299
  • Network effects: lawyers refer to other lawyers — 1 good firm = pipeline
  • Max is already a customer of this segment (his own patent filing)
  • Patent 75 §8B.4 literally says "legal and evidentiary attestation" as embodiment
Why NOT first
  • 3-9 month sales cycles (vs. 1-4 weeks for cannabis operator)
  • Conservative buying committees — partners vote
  • Malpractice exposure raises stakes if product breaks
  • SOC 2 Type II required for mid-firms ($30-50K, 6-12mo)
  • Entrenched incumbents (DocuSign, iManage, NetDocuments, Relativity)
  • Product must mature in cannabis first or fail publicly in legal

Strategic sequencing: cannabis proves the product in a forgiving vertical where operators tolerate early-stage rough edges in exchange for genuine value. Legal is zero-tolerance — a single failure becomes a malpractice insurance claim AND a reputation scar. Let the product harden 6-9 months in cannabis, THEN expand into legal with a confidence-tested foundation.

4 · FRE 902(13)/(14) self-authenticating certificate

The certificate that replaces a records-custodian deposition.

Federal Rules of Evidence 902 lists categories of evidence that are self-authenticating — admissible without extrinsic evidence of authenticity. Subsections 902(13) and 902(14) were added in 2017 specifically for electronic records:

FRE 902(13) — Certified Records Generated by an Electronic Process or System

"A record generated by an electronic process or system that produces an accurate result, as shown by a certification of a qualified person that complies with the certification requirements of Rule 902(11) or (12)."

FRE 902(14) — Certified Data Copied from an Electronic Device, Storage Medium, or File

"Data copied from an electronic device, storage medium, or file, if authenticated by a process of digital identification, as shown by a certification of a qualified person that complies with the certification requirements of Rule 902(11) or (12)."

What this means for BrandrAI: if we produce a properly-formed certificate signed by a qualified person, declaring that the record was generated by BrandrAI's cryptographic-hash system with a reliable process, the record is admissible at trial without a witness testifying to its authenticity. This saves 1-3 hours of deposition time per exhibit + eliminates opposing-counsel challenges over authentication.

4.2 Who is a "qualified person"?

FRE 902(13)/(14) requires the certificate to be signed by a "qualified person." Courts have interpreted this as someone with personal knowledge of the process producing the record. Three options:

OptionSignerPros / Cons
A. BrandrAI CTO attestsDang Tien Ngoc (as CTO, in his capacity)Dang has personal knowledge of the system. Most defensible. But requires Dang's availability if challenged at trial (unlikely given 902 is self-authenticating).
B. Client firm designee attestsFirm's records-custodian, authorized under an MSABest for law firms — their own person attests. BrandrAI provides the technical appendix + hash values; the person certifies the process per the MSA.
C. HybridBoth a BrandrAI technical declaration + the firm's designeeStrongest evidentiarily. Use for high-stakes litigation.

Default implementation: Option B with Option C upgrade available. Product generates a PDF certificate with all technical fields pre-filled; the client firm designee signs the declaration. BrandrAI provides a separate pre-signed technical declaration (Option A) for the firm to attach as an exhibit if desired. Billed at 2 credits per certificate; Option C adds 1 credit for BrandrAI's concurrent declaration.

6 · Sales playbook per segment

Specific opening lines + objection-handling per segment.

P0 — Patent / IP firms
Opening: "Can I ask how you currently timestamp inventor disclosures? Most firms use PDF date fields, email receipts, or DocuSign — none of which are forensically defensible if inventorship is challenged. We built the primitive that makes it defensible."
Objection — "We use DocuSign already": "DocuSign logs when YOU signed. It can't prove the inventor created the document on a specific date. Our system timestamps + hashes at the moment of receipt, with a publicly-verifiable record. It's not the same thing."
Demo hook: show Max's own patent filing timestamps + the verify URL. "This is what your inventors' disclosures could look like."
P1 — IP + commercial litigation
Opening: "How many hours a year does your firm spend authenticating exhibits under FRE 901 — having custodians swear to records? FRE 902(13) lets a self-authenticating certificate replace that deposition. We build the certificate."
Objection — "We already use Relativity": "Relativity handles document review. We handle authentication of the records before they enter your review universe. Different layer. Complementary, not competitive."
Demo hook: drag-and-drop a signed agreement onto the verify page; show the hash match; explain this is what the FRE 902(13) certificate proves in court.
P2 — Cannabis-adjacent firms
Opening: "Your cannabis clients are already using BrandrAI for their COA verification. You're the law firm helping them with their licensing, regulatory correspondence, and operator contracts. Objection — "Our clients don't ask for this": "They will next quarter when the DEA hearing concludes July 15 and interstate commerce starts being a real question. Brands with verifiable contract trails will have a massive advantage in interstate enforcement. Early adopter firms lead that wave."

10 · Risks + mitigations

What can go wrong and how to avoid it.

Risk: Court challenges FRE 902(13) certificate in landmark case, finds our process insufficient

Mitigation: use NIST-certified SHA-256, cite FIPS 180-4 explicitly in the certificate, partner with a known timestamp authority (Symantec/DigiCert) for RFC 3161 timestamps. Consult with evidence-law professor or litigator before launch to stress-test the certificate language.

Risk: Product failure causes lost evidence in a real case

Mitigation: immutable append-only storage (S3 object lock), geo-redundant backups, automated daily integrity checks. MSA disclaims liability for lost evidence except in cases of gross negligence, capped at 12 months of fees. Professional liability insurance policy specific to tech-for-legal.

Risk: State bar association objects to marketing language

Mitigation: avoid "guaranteed admissibility" claims; use "compliant with FRE 902(13) requirements" language. Run marketing copy by a legal ethics advisor before publication. Don't make claims about legal outcomes.

Risk: Competitor (DocuSign, LogixCertified) enters with similar claims

Mitigation: patent 75 (filing before May 1) specifically covers §8B.4 legal-evidentiary embodiments. Early filing = competitive moat. Trademark "BrandrAI Proof for Legal" promptly.

Risk: SOC 2 audit finds major gaps

Mitigation: engage Drata/Vanta 3 months before audit for readiness assessment. Fix gaps iteratively. Have Type I attestation before Type II audit.
