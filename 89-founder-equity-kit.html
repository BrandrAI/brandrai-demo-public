7 · How the 2% performance options work

Mechanics of the milestone-unlocked grants.

MilestoneGrant amountMechanism
$10K MRR50,000 shares (0.5%)Board resolution grants ISO option at FMV on date of grant; 4-yr vest from grant date
$50K MRR50,000 shares (0.5%)Same mechanism as above; triggered by first month hitting $50K MRR
$200K MRR50,000 shares (0.5%)Same mechanism
Series A close50,000 shares (0.5%)Triggered by first priced equity round of ≥ $2M

Key mechanics:

  • Grants are ISO options (incentive stock options), not additional common stock. Dang exercises to convert into common.
  • Each grant has its own 4-year vesting schedule starting on the grant date (NOT backdated). Rationale: performance options reward future performance from the grant date forward, not historical work.
  • Strike price = fair market value on date of grant (set by a 409A valuation at the time, or a board good-faith determination for early grants).
  • Grants are conditional — if milestone is achieved, board must approve the grant; board cannot arbitrarily refuse if the milestone has been verifiably achieved. Document this clearly in the eventual option plan.
  • MRR measurement: defined as Monthly Recurring Revenue from paid subscriptions, calculated on a trailing 30-day basis, audited by the company's accountant. Defined precisely in the option plan to avoid disputes.
  • If Dang departs before a milestone is achieved, that tranche does not vest or grant — options are forfeited.

Tax treatment: ISOs with proper holding periods get long-term capital gains on the spread at sale. If Dang is employed via an EOR in Vietnam, ISO status may not apply (ISOs require US employment). Alternative structure: NSOs (non-qualified stock options) — ordinary income on spread at exercise, but no US employment requirement. Your corporate counsel + tax advisor should confirm the right structure when the stock option plan is adopted (pre-first-milestone).
