1 · What a provisional actually is

A priority-date placeholder. Nothing more, nothing less.

What it DOES do
  • Establishes a priority date — anything published after this date cannot be cited against you as prior art for the inventions disclosed
  • Gives you 12 months to convert to a non-provisional (or PCT international)
  • Lets you write "patent pending" on marketing
  • Preserves international filing rights under the Paris Convention
What it DOES NOT do
  • Does NOT get examined — no examiner reads it
  • Does NOT require formal claims (though claims are strongly recommended)
  • Does NOT grant you a patent — only the non-provisional can
  • Does NOT require prior art search
  • Does NOT require an inventor oath/declaration
  • Does NOT cover anything not disclosed in it — what's in is protected, what's out is unprotected

The single most important rule: whatever you disclose in the provisional is what you can claim priority to when you convert. Anything material you add at conversion time will NOT get the provisional's priority date. So the disclosure must be complete enough to cover all likely claim scope. What MUST be in every provisional filing.

What's explicitly NOT required: claims, abstract, oath/declaration, prior art statement, assignment. You CAN include claims (and you should for anything important — see next section) but they are not legally mandatory.

3 · Best-practice additions

Strongly recommended even though not legally required.

A · Claims (2-10 broad claims per application)

Even though not required, claims anchor the invention and discipline the disclosure. Write 1-3 broad independent claims + 5-10 dependent claims per filing. You can amend at conversion, but claims filed today create a clear priority-date record.

B · Abstract (150 words max)

Plain-English single-paragraph summary. Not required but examiners use it at conversion and it helps counsel later.

C · Cross-reference to related applications

If any of these 3 filings relate to prior filings (e.g. the existing cannabis provisional), reference the application number. Matters for priority chaining.

D · Statement re: federal R&D funding

Boilerplate: "No federal government funding was used in developing this invention." Only needed if there IS federal funding; omit otherwise.

E · Drawings with numbered reference labels

Each figure needs numbered elements (e.g. "100 = application server, 102 = database") and the specification must reference those numbers. Informal drawings (hand-sketched, screenshots) are acceptable at provisional; only non-provisional requires formal USPTO-compliant drafting.

F · "Best mode" disclosure

Post-AIA this is no longer a validity defense, but disclosing your actual preferred implementation (model names, library versions, exact parameters) is good practice and strengthens enablement.

G · Alternative embodiments explicitly enumerated

For each technology choice (hash function, LLM provider, storage backend), list alternatives. Prevents accusations of overclaiming and preserves broader claim scope at conversion.

4 · Small vs. micro entity

Check if you qualify for micro-entity — 75% fee reduction.

Large entity
$325 / filing
Default — most companies
Small entity
$130 / filing
Companies < 500 employees, individual inventors, non-profits
Micro entity (GOAL)
$65 / filing
Small entity + additional income/filing tests

Micro-entity qualification (37 CFR 1.29), all four must be true:

  1. Qualifies as a small entity
  2. Has NOT been named as an inventor on more than four previously-filed patent applications (excluding foreign, provisional, or applications assigned to previous employer as condition of employment)
  3. Gross income in the previous calendar year ≤ 3× the US median household income (~$206K for 2024; check current threshold)
  4. Has NOT assigned, granted, or conveyed (and is not under obligation to do so) a license or ownership interest to an entity that doesn't also qualify as a micro entity

Max + Dang likely BOTH qualify as micro-entity today as individual inventors pre-incorporation. Total 3 filings at micro rate = ~$195. If you miss micro and just qualify as small = ~$390. Either way it's < $1K. Do not overthink this — claim small entity and file. Claim micro only if you can firmly check all four boxes.

  2. Draft claims — minimum 3 independent + 10 dependent per patent. Method / system / non-transitory computer-readable medium format.
  3. Draft abstract — 150 words per patent, plain-English.

  5. Determine entity status — small or micro. Likely micro today as individual inventors.

Strongly recommended (worth the extra hour)

  2. Explicit "alternative embodiment" listing at end of each spec, separate from the main text.
  3. Disclosure timeline appendix — list every public mention of the invention (pitches, demos, changelog, website copy). Critical for prior-art-by-self analysis.

  2. "Person of ordinary skill" statement defining target audience
  3. Statement re: federal funding (if none, can omit)

9 · DIY filing steps — USPTO Patent Center

The actual clicks to file each provisional.

  1. 1 · Create USPTO.gov account

    Go to patentcenter.uspto.gov. Register for a MyUSPTO account with 2FA. Link a USPTO.gov account as "Sponsored User" or "Unsponsored Public." For individual inventor filings, unsponsored is fine.

  2. 2 · Assemble filing documents (1 PDF per section is cleanest)
    • specification.pdf — the full written description (title, field, background, summary, detailed description, claims, abstract)
    • drawings.pdf — all figures, one per page, black & white preferred, numbered labels
    • sb-16.pdf (optional) — Patent Center auto-generates this from the online form; you don't need to pre-fill
  3. 3 · Click "New Submission" → "Provisional Application"

    Patent Center walks you through a wizard. Select "Utility — Provisional" as the application type.

  4. 4 · Enter bibliographic data
    • Title of invention
    • Inventor(s): full legal name + residence city/state/country for each
    • Correspondence address (you, for now; update when counsel is retained)
    • Entity status: small or micro (check the appropriate box; you're attesting under penalty of 37 CFR 1.27/1.29)
    • Cross-reference to prior application (cannabis only — list the earlier provisional's app number)
  5. 5 · Upload documents

    Specification goes under "Specification (SPEC)." Drawings under "Drawings (DRW)." Patent Center will flag PDF issues (font embedding, OCR, sizing) — fix locally and re-upload.

  6. 6 · Review, pay, submit

    Pay via credit card or USPTO deposit account. File accepts up to 11:59 PM Eastern on the filing day. You get an immediate e-filing receipt with an Application Number and a Filing Date — that Filing Date is your priority date. Screenshot it, print it, email yourself a copy.

  7. 7 · Repeat for each of the 3 patents

    Each is a separate submission with a separate fee. Do them back-to-back in the same session so entity-status answers stay consistent.

⚡ Time estimate on filing day

~30 minutes per application via Patent Center. ~90 minutes total for 3 applications. Do it in one sitting with a checklist printed.

10 · After filing

The 12-month clock starts the day you file.

  • Save everything. Filing receipts, app numbers, filing dates, all documents. Create a patents/ folder with subfolders per application.
  • Calendar the 12-month deadline (e.g. Apr 28 2026 filing → Apr 28 2027 conversion deadline). Set reminders at 10, 9, 6, 3, and 1 months out.
  • Do NOT publicly disclose anything NOT in the provisional without adding a supplemental provisional or accepting it as prior art against your eventual non-provisional.
  • Retain patent counsel for non-provisional conversion — this is where Edison Law Group (or equivalent) is worth the money. Claim drafting for the non-provisional is where real patent prosecution skill matters.
  • File PCT application at month 11 if you want international rights — preserves priority to all Paris Convention countries for another 18 months.
  • Post-incorporation: once BrandrAI, Inc. is formed (Clerky), execute nunc pro tunc assignment from Max + Dang (individually) to BrandrAI, Inc., and record the assignment with USPTO within 3 months of signature.
Critical reminder

A provisional that's never converted expires into the void at 12 months. No extensions, no appeals, no grace. If the 12-month conversion deadline passes without a non-provisional filing (or a PCT), everything in the provisional becomes public-domain prior art against your own later attempts. Calendar it. Twice.
