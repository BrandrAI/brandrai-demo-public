The problem

“Mail a letter to yourself” doesn't work.

Poor man's copyright — mailing yourself an envelope to prove authorship — has been legally discredited for decades. Postmarks are forgeable. Envelopes can be opened and resealed. Courts stopped accepting it in the 1990s.

But the need remains real. Writers, inventors, researchers, photographers, lawyers, and engineers all need some way to prove a document existed on a given date. The actual solutions today are either absurdly expensive (notary + attorney, $200+) or niche (WGA registration, Bitcoin timestamping services with no UX).

The infrastructure exists. The user experience doesn't. That's the opportunity.

How it works

Four steps. Legally defensible.

Every BrandrAI Proof timestamp produces a court-ready PDF certificate, a public verify page with a QR code, and a cryptographic hash you can anchor anywhere.

1

Upload

PDF, Word, image, video, dataset, code archive. Anything up to 100 MB. Drag and drop or API.

2

Hash + timestamp

SHA-256 hash computed at upload. Bound to a UTC timestamp from a trusted time source. Optional on-chain anchoring via public blockchain.

3

AI analysis

Structured data extracted (authors, dates, titles, key entities). Basic integrity check — does the document appear tampered?

4

Certificate + QR

Download a signed PDF certificate. Get a public verify URL. Print the QR on your manuscript, contract, canvas, or packaging.
